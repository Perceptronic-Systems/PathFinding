Path Finding Demo

Imagine you are pouring water onto a specific tile of a grid, like a checkered floor, and watching it spread out. A flood-fill pathfinding algorithm works exactly like that water: it starts at your beginning point and flows outward, filling every adjacent open tile one by one, step by step. As the "water" expands, it remembers how many steps it took to reach each tile from the start, completely ignoring walls or obstacles it can't flow through. Once the spreading water finally touches your destination tile, the algorithm simply traces the numbers backward—like following a trail of breadcrumbs from the destination back to the source—to reveal the shortest, most direct path through the grid.
